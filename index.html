Urban Agriculture Ordinance



The City of Gainesville supports efforts to improve our local food system and increase access to fresh, healthy food for all Gainesville neighbors. We invite you to provide feedback on a proposed draft for an Urban Agriculture Ordinance for our Gainesville that aims to support urban agricultural practices within our City limits.

If adopted, this ordinance will be included as an amendment to the City of Gainesville's Land Development Code. You can provide your feedback on the draft ordinance language and the concept of urban agriculture in Gainesville by clicking the button below.

The platform will be open from January 17th to February 21st, 2020. We look forward to our neighbors’ feedback and appreciate your participation in the City’s decision making processes.

Survey on Homelessness in Gainesville



This survey has been created by University of Florida students interning at the City of Gainesville. We aim to use this survey to better understand community members' feelings towards people experiencing homelessness in the greater Gainesville area.

The survey results will inform our creation of a proposed awareness campaign that aims to address common misperceptions of homelessness. The results will also inform suggestions for possible resources that could be offered to assist people experiencing homelessness. The information gathered through this survey will be shared with local providers who are currently serving people experiencing homelessness. This survey is anonymous and will take 5-7 minutes to complete.

GCRA Final Draft Reinvestment Plan Workshop

The GCRA will be holding a public workshop to prioritize specific initiatives that will make up the 10 Year Reinvestment Plan.

At the workshop, the Consolidated Reinvestment Plan draft document will be presented. The public will have an opportunity to validate its findings and help prioritize the investments into the district for the next 10 years.

The A. Quinn Jones Center is located at 1108 NW 7th Avenue in the Fifth Avenue Neighborhood. Parking is available in the A. Quinn Jones Center parking lot, with additional spaces across the street at the A. Quinn Jones Museum and Cultural Center. Refreshments will be provided.

Wednesday, February 19th, 2020 from 5:30-7:30pm

Location: A. Quinn Jones Center
